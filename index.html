Prototyping Studio

Background


In the prototyping studio, every week we learnt a different type of prototyping techniques. From paper prototyping to model, video, behavioral, website, mobile prototyping and then to physical prototyping with Arduino and 3D printing. It was a fun ride!



Design Process


Each week we practiced a new prototyping technique. I created, tested, and iterated. I like talking a product idea before it is well formed, so I could collect feedback early. I also use a storyboard, a sketch, or a low-fidelity artifact to communicate the idea. Then with others' input, I go back and refine it. Through the iterative process I found my idea gets evolved and matured.

Read more about the design process at my Medium prototyping blog below.



Gioia

Outcome


My biggest takeaway from the course could be summarized with 2 Lean UX principles: making over analysis and continuous discovery. Instead of guessing and thinking hard on your own, make the idea concrete by start building it, then get out of the building to collect people's feedback. Next, iterate and refine your ideas by continuously engaging users in the design process.


Another outcome would be, one of my projects - arduino music box - got me approached by a EEWeb editor, she would like to feature the project after seeing my documentating video on youtube. I am glad people find it interesting.

Design of TV Applications

Background


The Opera TV Store is an HTML5-based storefront displaying web applications made for TV and run from the cloud. It gives the user a comfortable, “lean-back” experience, using a standard TV remote control to navigate, select and launch apps easily. Like other stores, it includes various TV apps spanning from video (the most popular category), music, social networking, games, news and information.

Role


My contributions are mainly focused on leading the outsourcing project of developing 10+ Chinese TV apps for Opera TV Store.

As the QA engineer in the TV team, I was responsible for moderating and publishing TV apps submitted by external developers or content partners. During this period I acquired TV apps design principles and understood potential developing issues. So when Opera TV Store planed to land on China TV market and required various local TV apps, I took charge and guided the development of various TV apps.

I also gave an speech on design of TV apps at Html5 Top conference 2012, to motivate HTML5 developers to build TV apps for Opera TV Store. The slides above is my presentation slides about design principles for TV screen.

Design Process


Back then in 2012, I did not know much about UX design. But I started by thinking about TV users and their context. I appreciated the offical Opera TV apps design principles and translated it into Chinese for our external designers and developers, to help the whole team understand how to design for TV users. I also did some user research and competitor analysis on what users wanted on their TV, and talked with our sales for reaching out the content providers. Then I worked closely with external designers and developers on various TV apps.



Outcome


Together with the content parnters and external developers, in 3 month we delivered more than 20+ TV apps for Opera TV store Chinese market. They are published and available on various TV sets such as Sony Bravia. Moreover, it gains us a few contracts with chinese TV manufactors with Opera TV store preinstalled in their TV.

Sphinx: A Browser-based Game Engine

Background


The lack of smooth experience in a mobile HTML5 game is always a big problem for game developers. To make the performance of web game like that of a native game and save game developers from worrying about performance issue, we build Sphinx, a game engine particularly optimized for HTML5 games.


Role


I was the product owner of Sphinx. With a 10-people team we managed to deliver a web platform, an Android emulator and a packaging platform for our users.


Checkout the website

Design Process


With clear objectives of building tools to promote the use of sphinx engine, I started the project by conducting competitive analysis on developer tools and defined the product scope, then generated and explored the alternatives of functional organization, with which I communicated often with stakeholders and internal users for feedback.

After that, I built different prototypes for user testing. After iterative testing, we selected a plan and moved to the detailed interface and graphic design to finalize the UI. Take emulator UI as an exmaple, the belowed images show the 3 different layout prototypes and finalized UI after user testing.




Outcome



Our team managed to deliver the whole set of development platform for Sphinx, but things changed when Opera switched to Blink engine. As an internal innotative product, the sphinx did not commercialize to a real product. Even though, we still learnt a lot through the process and gained some users when publishing the platform.


Opera Mini 7.8.2 Shipment

Background


Opera Mini is a lightweight mobile browser with 250 million monthly active user. Its Chinese version is derived from the international one and has been adapted to meet local needs. Also the business model has been updated too. For confidential reason I will simply describe the delivered part of my work.

My contribution


I was the lead product manager of Opera Mini Chinese version and managed to ship version 7.8.2 with key updates and within schedule.


Checkout the website

Design Process


As the lead product manager, first I planed for the next product iteration and discussed the potential shippable features with other product managers. Then I prioritized the features and added to the product backlog, with which I reported to the executive team and described the value delivered. After finalizing the features, I drove the overall product development by coordinating tasks and resources within multi-functional teams. At last I worked with marketing team to ship the new version. Then based on the feedback, we delivered the final product after positive feedback. The left screen feature increased the revenue by a few percent.

Opera Browser Extension Development

Background


Extensions are small code packages that you can add to your browser to extend its functionality. It is built using open standards (HTML5, CSS, JavaScript) and supported APIs. At the time I joined Opera, there is 718 extensions and 80 Speeddial extensions (@2011.07.12).

Role


Back in year 2011 I developed 5+ speed-dial extensions for Opera desktop browser. Among them Sina Weibo extension reached 200,000 downloads.


Sina Weibo extension is developed to pull messages from my account and saved me effort of checking my timeline now and then. It updated its content in a pre-defined time cycle and showed a notification bar if new message coming in.


Checkout the Sina Weibo Extension

Design Process


Initially I joined Opera software as Desktop QA, after one month product orientation, I took the initiative and started to build extensions for Opera Desktop. Firstly I defined what I wanted to build. As Weibo (chinese Twitter) started to gain popularity, I noticed the demand for a convenient way to check its update. To enhance the browsing experience without user refreshing the website for updates, I chose to pushes notifications to users. I decided to work on speeddial extensions so users could access the information easier without any click. Then I defined the features and tested internally in our office. After that, I did a few iterations and then submit it through my personal developer account on Opera extension store.

After publishing I got some users' comments. I responsed and updated the extension a few times.

Outcome


The extension turned out to be a success: it got a few 5 stars reviews and lots of downloads. Then I was approached by my colleague in Norway to promote the extension. Moreover, on Chinese user community forum, people liked it and shared it happily.

From this project, I found myself enjoying the role of designing a product, it enabled me to make an impact at a high level. Knowing that many users are relying on my product also give me a strong sense of accomplishment.

Master Thesis: Context Aware Framework

Background


Context aware means applications can both sense and react based on the environment. But in reality the commercial context aware mobile applications are still limited, mostly are those using location information to find nearby friends or services available in the market.

This happened mainly because of a lack of an effective mechanism to collect and share contextual information. Nowadays, user context data are scattered around due to the significant growing in smart phones, mobile apps, sensing technology and social networking, how to combine these data to leverage information about users are important and promptly needed, which motivated the topic of my thesis: Applying Personal Cloud in Existing Mobile Context Aware Framework.

Behind the Scenes


In my thesis project I reviewed the approaches in existing context aware platforms, and reformed the context aware framework by absorbing previous fundamental technical aspects into personal cloud. By using a user-centric context collector approach, I enhanced the overall applications intelligence and reduced the privacy and security problem. A prototype application has also been developed for the purpose of evaluating the Context widget and obtaining hands-on experience of building a context-aware application. The function of the mobile application is fetching the content from a web site for film on TV today. And based on historical selected liked film database, contexts on user film preferences are auto-generated and context aware adaption is allowed for updating the film list order based on its reasoning logic.
